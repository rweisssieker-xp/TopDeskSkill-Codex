# Operator Handbook

Audience: service desk and back-office operators.

## Daily Workflow

1. Review assigned queue and near-breach tickets.
2. Check caller, branch, category, priority, asset, and status.
3. Add clear action notes.
4. Keep public replies separate from internal notes.
5. Link knowledge, assets, changes, or related incidents where useful.
6. Close only when resolution and required fields are complete.

## AI/KI Suggestions

Do:

- Review confidence and reason.
- Accept, edit, or reject suggestions.
- Use override reasons.
- Check citations before sending draft replies.

Do not:

- Send generated replies without review.
- Put internal notes into customer-visible messages.
- Let AI close tickets or change sensitive priorities without approval.

## Escalation

Escalate when:

- SLA breach risk is high.
- Multiple similar incidents suggest major incident.
- Sensitive category is involved.
- Required asset/person/branch data is missing.

## Quality Rules

- Use consistent categories.
- Avoid vague closure notes.
- Link knowledge when resolution is reusable.
- Reopen with reason and update status history.
